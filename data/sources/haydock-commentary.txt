[Genesis 1:1]
In the beginning God created heaven, and earth.
Ver. 1. In the beginning. This refers to the beginning of time. God, in his eternal nature, decided to form the universe from nothing. The phrase highlights the absolute power of the Creator, laying the foundation for all subsequent christological typology where the Word (Logos) acts as the agent of creation.
See Catechism 290 on Trinitarian creation.
The Hebrew word used here is 'Bereshit', and 'Elohim' for God, which being a plural noun used with a singular verb, early church fathers saw as a hint of the Trinity.

[Genesis 1:2]
And the earth was void and empty, and darkness was upon the face of the deep; and the spirit of God moved over the waters.
Ver. 2. Void and empty. The earth was a formless mass. The Spirit moving over the waters prefigures the sacrament of Baptism, where the Holy Spirit brings new spiritual life from water.
See Catechism 292 on the action of the Holy Spirit.
The word translated 'moved over' (merachephet) carries the nuance of a bird hovering over its young, protecting and nurturing the chaotic elements.
